# Environmental Computation (EC)
> Beta 0.9.0 | Thesis by Yujun Kong | Stevens Institute of Technology / China Academy of Art

## Overview
**Environmental Computation (EC)** is a human-centered, environment-feature-driven computational paradigm. It integrates distributed embedded nodes, low‑latency networks, and embedded AI to transform physical environments into intelligent, context‑aware computing spaces.

Unlike traditional smart city and smart home systems that operate in isolated silos, EC treats the **environment itself as the core computing context**, enabling real‑time collaboration between physical locations, digital services, and end users.

## Core Concept
EC is defined as an **Environment‑Feature‑Driven Computational Methodology**:
- Every environment has **one exclusive pivotal feature** (e.g., business district, campus, public square).
- Computation is bounded by location, user role, and contextual needs.
- Embedded AI enhances perception, decision‑making, and natural interaction.

## Key Application Scenarios
- **Smart Business Complex**: Proactive, context‑aware service recommendations aligned with the environment’s business feature.
- **Cultural & Historical Exploration**: Triggered digital storytelling and insights based on user attention in public spaces.
- **Public Safety & Emergency Response**: Real‑time anomaly detection and coordinated rescue via environment‑deployed EC nodes.

## Necessity & Feasibility
- **Necessity**: Addresses fragmentation, poor interoperability, and lack of context awareness in existing smart systems.
- **Feasibility**: Built on mature modern stacks: edge computing, IoT sensors, low‑latency networks, and lightweight AI models.
